    What do the results mean to me?

    Knowing how I work and interact in a professional environment; I feel these results are very accurate. I sat down and discussed the results of the Myers-Briggs test with my fiancé and she agreed that these personality traits are accurate. I like to be in control of a situation, because I feel I can deliver the best results. I also know when to relinquish control, if I analyse the situation and feel that what I am doing is not going to be delivering the best results. This situation usually occurs when a task is beyond my experience or my knowledge, I’ll be the first to admit that it is out of my depth.

    I believe the People Master attribute to be the most accurate from this personality test, particularly in regards to being stress resistant. I am accustomed to working in a high stress and high pressure environment and I was able to do so with ease, whilst also maintaining an exceptionally high standard of work.

    The results of my learning style I mostly agree with. I am a very visual learner, and a very hands on sort of person. However, there is a sentence that describes the tactile part of my learning as not being able to sit still and take frequent breaks that I disagree with. I am very much accustom to putting my head down and getting the work done, and rarely take a full lunch break during the day.

    The results of the Gallup Strength finder are used at my current workplace. These results are taken seriously when forming teams as they take your strengths and weaknesses into consideration. I take the results of this very seriously, as I feel it describes my professional personality and my private personality.


    How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?

    For me, I like to be in control or maintaining control. So if my team is straying of course, not making deadlines, making mistakes, etc I’ll be attempting to keep them on track. At the moment, I am learning a new role in my profession, so my focus is all on learning and listening, not so much trying to take control.

    How should take this into account when forming a team?

    When forming a team I think it’s important for one person (or two) to be able to dictate the flow of the workload and manage the outcomes of each member appropriately. In my previous experience from working in the Data Centre, I was the lead of my shift for several years and a senior employee for most of my tenure. I had a lot of success in completing tasks and rarely needing to handover tasks that were scheduled to be completed on my shift as I had managed my/my team’s time effectively.
