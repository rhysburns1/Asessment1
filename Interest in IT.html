I’ve had an interest in IT since I was about 11-12 years old. My grandparents got a computer, and at the time it was top of the line and cost them a lot of money. I would use it almost every day to play WoW and Runescape, and eventually after I gave both of those up, I turned to playing real time strategy games instead. As I was getting older and had a part time job, I decided that I’d try my hand at building a PC and with the help of a family friend who was nice enough to give me a few parts, I was able to build my computer.


In my spare time, I run a small online community that offers support to new PC gamers, by providing advice and assistance with anything related to online gaming. I’ve built this off Discord and have had a lot of success in providing members with the help and support they need over the last few years.


At this time, I am working in IT, specifically as a Network Security Administrator. I started my career as a Level 1 Support Technician and progressed to a Level 2 Support Technician in the same company. I left this role and started a role as a Level 1 Data Centre (DC) Support Specialist, then a Level 2 DC Support Specialist and finally a Level 3 DC Support Specialist. I left my career for 12 months to travel, and upon my return, applied for a role in the same company I had left 12 months prior, as a Level 1 Network Security Administrator.
